Cattle Management Guide

Essential practices for successful cattle farming

Last Updated: March 14, 2024 | 15 min read

Basics of Cattle Management

Successful cattle management requires attention to various aspects including nutrition, health care, breeding, and housing. Understanding these fundamentals is crucial for maintaining a healthy and productive herd.

Essential Requirements

  • Adequate pasture/feed
  • Clean water supply
  • Proper shelter
  • Healthcare facilities
  • Record keeping system

Management Goals

  • Optimal growth rates
  • Good reproductive performance
  • Disease prevention
  • Cost-effective production

Nutrition Management

Feed Requirements

  • Quality forage/pasture
  • Balanced concentrate feed
  • Mineral supplements
  • Clean water access
  • Age-specific nutrition

Feeding Schedule

  • Regular feeding times
  • Monitor feed intake
  • Adjust rations as needed
  • Seasonal considerations

Health Management

Physical Appearance
Monitor body condition, coat quality, and general alertness
Behavior
Observe feeding, movement, and social interactions
Vital Signs
Check temperature, respiration, and heart rate regularly
Production Parameters
Track growth rates, milk production, or weight gain

Preventive Care

  • Regular vaccinations
  • Parasite control
  • Hoof care
  • Dental checks

Breeding Management

Breeding Program

  • Select quality breeding stock
  • Monitor heat cycles
  • Time breeding properly
  • Keep breeding records
  • Plan genetic improvements

Calving Management

  • Prepare calving area
  • Monitor pregnant cows
  • Assist when necessary
  • Care for newborn calves

Housing and Facilities

Shelter Requirements

  • Adequate space per animal
  • Proper ventilation
  • Weather protection
  • Easy cleaning access

Facility Maintenance

  • Regular cleaning
  • Repair damaged structures
  • Update equipment
  • Maintain fencing
