Housing System Fundamentals

Proper livestock housing is essential for animal welfare, productivity, and efficient farm management.

Key Considerations

  • Animal welfare
  • Environmental control
  • Labor efficiency
  • Cost effectiveness
  • Biosecurity measures

Types of Housing Systems

Intensive Systems

  • Confined housing
  • Climate controlled
  • High management input
  • Maximum space efficiency

Semi-Intensive Systems

  • Combined indoor/outdoor
  • Seasonal flexibility
  • Moderate management
  • Balance of space and control

Extensive Systems

  • Pasture-based
  • Natural environment
  • Lower management input
  • Large space requirement

Species-Specific Requirements

Space Requirements

Species Indoor Space (m²/animal) Outdoor Space (m²/animal)
Cattle 6-10 20-30
Sheep/Goats 1.5-2 4-6
Pigs 1-3 3-5
