Vaccination Programs

Essential vaccination schedules for livestock health

Last Updated: Mar 14, 2024 | 15 min read

Importance of Vaccination

Vaccination is a crucial preventive measure that helps protect livestock from various diseases, reducing mortality rates and improving overall herd health.

Key Benefits

  • Disease prevention
  • Reduced mortality rates
  • Improved productivity
  • Cost-effective healthcare

Species-Specific Schedules

Cattle

  • Brucellosis: 4-8 months
  • BVD: 6 months
  • IBR: Annual
  • Blackleg: 6 months

Sheep & Goats

  • Clostridial: 2-3 months
  • Pasteurella: Annual
  • Foot rot: Seasonal
  • CLA: 6 months

Pigs

  • Mycoplasma: 3-4 weeks
  • PRRS: Pre-breeding
  • E. coli: Pre-farrowing
  • Swine flu: Seasonal

Vaccination Best Practices

Storage and Handling

  • Maintain proper temperature (2-8°C)
  • Check expiration dates
  • Use sterile equipment
  • Follow manufacturer guidelines

Administration

  • Use proper injection sites
  • Follow dosage instructions
  • Record all vaccinations
  • Monitor for reactions

Record Keeping

Essential Records

  • Vaccination dates
  • Product information
  • Batch numbers
  • Animal identification
  • Next due dates

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Critical Errors

  • Improper storage temperature
  • Missed booster shots
  • Wrong injection technique
  • Poor timing of vaccination
