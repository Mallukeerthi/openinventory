Tractor Maintenance Guide

Essential maintenance tips for your tractor

Last Updated: March 14, 2024 | 15 min read

Daily Maintenance Checks

Regular daily maintenance is crucial for keeping your tractor in optimal working condition and preventing costly repairs.

Before Starting

  • Check engine oil level
  • Inspect coolant level
  • Check fuel level
  • Inspect air filter
  • Check tire pressure

During Operation

  • Monitor gauges
  • Listen for unusual sounds
  • Watch for leaks
  • Check brake response

Maintenance Schedule

Every 50 Hours
Check battery, lubricate fittings, clean air filter
Every 100 Hours
Change engine oil and filter, check hydraulic fluid
Every 200 Hours
Adjust valve clearance, check fuel injectors
Every 500 Hours
Change transmission fluid, replace fuel filters

Engine Maintenance

Oil and Filters

  • Use recommended oil grade
  • Change oil at specified intervals
  • Replace filters regularly
  • Check for contamination
  • Monitor oil pressure

Cooling System

  • Check coolant level daily
  • Inspect radiator fins
  • Test thermostat operation
  • Clean cooling system annually

Transmission and Hydraulics

Transmission Care

  • Check fluid levels
  • Inspect for leaks
  • Monitor unusual noises
  • Service at intervals

Hydraulic System

  • Check hydraulic oil
  • Replace filters
  • Test system pressure
  • Inspect hoses

Tire and Wheel Maintenance

Tire Care

  • Check pressure regularly
  • Inspect for wear
  • Rotate as needed
  • Balance properly
  • Clean thoroughly

Wheel Maintenance

  • Check wheel bolts
  • Inspect bearings
  • Look for cracks
  • Clean regularly
