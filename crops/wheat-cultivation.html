Introduction to Wheat Cultivation

Wheat is one of the world's most important cereal crops, providing essential nutrients to billions of people. This guide will walk you through the complete process of wheat cultivation, from soil preparation to storage.

Key Points

  • Wheat is adaptable to various climates
  • Requires moderate rainfall or irrigation
  • Growing season: 100-150 days
  • Optimal temperature: 15-25°C

Soil Preparation

Proper soil preparation is crucial for successful wheat cultivation. The soil should be well-draining and have a pH between 6.0 and 7.0.

1

Deep Plowing

Plow the field to a depth of 20-25 cm to ensure good root development. This helps break up compacted soil and incorporate any crop residues.

2

Leveling

Level the field properly to ensure uniform water distribution and prevent waterlogging.

3

Adding Organic Matter

Incorporate well-rotted manure or compost to improve soil structure and fertility.

Sowing Techniques

Proper sowing ensures good germination and establishes a strong foundation for your wheat crop.

Sowing Guidelines

  • Seed rate: 100-125 kg/hectare
  • Row spacing: 20-22.5 cm
  • Depth: 5-6 cm
  • Best time: Early morning or late afternoon
