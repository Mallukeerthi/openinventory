Core Organic Principles

Understanding the fundamental principles of organic farming

Last Updated: March 14, 2024 | 15 min read

What is Organic Farming?

Organic farming is a holistic approach to agriculture that focuses on producing food while sustaining the ecological balance and promoting biodiversity. It emphasizes the use of natural processes and materials while avoiding synthetic inputs.

Key Definition

Organic farming is an agricultural system that integrates cultural, biological, and mechanical practices that foster cycling of resources, promote ecological balance, and conserve biodiversity.

Core Principles

Health

Organic agriculture should sustain and enhance the health of soil, plants, animals, humans, and the planet as one and indivisible.

  • Maintain soil health through natural methods
  • Produce nutritious, high-quality food
  • Avoid use of fertilizers, pesticides, drugs, and food additives
  • Support animal welfare

Ecology

Organic agriculture should be based on living ecological systems and cycles, work with them, emulate them, and help sustain them.

  • Work within natural ecological systems
  • Maintain and enhance biodiversity
  • Protect environmental resources
  • Practice responsible resource use

Fairness

Organic agriculture should build on relationships that ensure fairness with regard to the common environment and life opportunities.

  • Ensure fair treatment of all involved
  • Practice social justice
  • Respect animal welfare
  • Use resources sustainably

Care

Organic agriculture should be managed in a precautionary and responsible manner to protect the health and well-being of current and future generations and the environment.

  • Practice preventive management
  • Reject unpredictable technologies
  • Make responsible decisions
  • Consider long-term impact

Implementation Guidelines

Practical Steps

  • Develop a comprehensive farm plan
  • Implement crop rotation systems
  • Use organic pest management strategies
  • Maintain detailed records
  • Build soil fertility naturally
  • Integrate livestock when possible
  • Create biodiversity zones
  • Practice water conservation

Benefits of Organic Farming

Environmental Benefits

  • Reduced pollution and soil erosion
  • Increased soil fertility
  • Water conservation
  • Enhanced biodiversity

Health Benefits

  • No synthetic pesticide residues
  • Higher nutrient content
  • Better taste and quality
  • Improved food safety
