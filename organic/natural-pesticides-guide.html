Natural Pesticides Guide

Making and using organic pesticides from natural ingredients

Last Updated: March 14, 2024 | 12 min read

Introduction to Natural Pesticides

Natural pesticides are environmentally friendly alternatives to synthetic chemicals, made from plant extracts, minerals, and other natural materials. They help control pests while maintaining ecological balance.

Safety First

Even though natural pesticides are organic, they should be handled with care. Always wear protective gear and follow application guidelines carefully.

Common Natural Pesticide Ingredients

Plant-Based Materials

  • Neem leaves and oil
  • Garlic and onions
  • Hot peppers
  • Chrysanthemum flowers
  • Marigold plants

Mineral-Based Materials

  • Diatomaceous earth
  • Kaolin clay
  • Baking soda
  • Rock dust

Other Natural Materials

  • Apple cider vinegar
  • Essential oils
  • Soap solutions
  • Vegetable oils

Natural Pesticide Recipes

Neem Oil Spray

A powerful all-purpose insecticide and fungicide.

  • 2 teaspoons neem oil
  • 1 teaspoon liquid soap
  • 1 quart warm water
  • Mix ingredients and spray on affected plants

Garlic-Chili Spray

Effective against soft-bodied insects and some fungal diseases.

  • 6-8 cloves of garlic
  • 1-2 hot chilies
  • 2 cups water
  • Blend, strain, and dilute before use

Herbal Insect Repellent

A gentle deterrent for various garden pests.

  • Fresh herbs (mint, lavender, rosemary)
  • 2 cups boiling water
  • Steep for 30 minutes
  • Strain and spray when cool

Application Guidelines

Best Practices

  • Apply early morning or late evening
  • Spray directly on affected areas
  • Reapply after rain
  • Test on small area first
  • Store properly labeled

Safety Measures

  • Wear protective gear
  • Keep away from children
  • Avoid contact with eyes
  • Wash hands after handling

Storage and Shelf Life

Storage Tips

Store natural pesticides in dark, cool places in clearly labeled containers. Most preparations last 1-2 weeks when properly stored.
